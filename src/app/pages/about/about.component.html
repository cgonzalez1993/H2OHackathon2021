Safety tips for avoiding harmful algal blooms

What is a harmful algal bloom?

Don't swim or come in direct contact with water that smells bad, looks discolored, or has scum on the surface. Don't let pets drink, play, or swim in water or eat algae. Stay out of the water if it has dead fish or other dead animals in or near it.

A harmful algal bloom can cause harm by: Producing toxins that can poison humans, fish, seabirds, aquatic animals, livestock, wildlife, and household pets (such as dogs) that are near the water, drink the water, or swim in the water. Causing illness when a person or animal eats fish or shellfish contaminated with algal toxins. Becoming dense enough to keep sunlight from reaching the lower depths of the water. Removing the oxygen from the water as it decomposes, starving fish and plants of oxygen, and damaging the local ecology.

Algal blooms can be toxic. Keep people and pets away from water that is green, scummy or smells bad.
